About

I’m a goal-oriented researcher in real-time & embedded systems, with work spanning scheduling theory, GPU/DNN scheduling, and edge-assisted autonomous driving. I like turning theory into dependable systems that are both predictable and efficient.

  • Focus: Scheduling Theory, Resource Management, Distributed Computing, Theoretical Analysis.
  • Platforms: ROS2, PyTorch, Linux, ONNX, TensorRT.
  • Tooling: Python/C/C++, CUDA, MATLAB, Git, Docker; workflow acceleration (pruning, quantization: FP32/FP16/INT8).
  • Outcome-driven: I combine modeling, implementation, and large-scale experiments to guide design choices for AVs.

Highlights

  • Reaction-latency analysis for ROS2 synchronization in edge-assisted AVs: derived safe bounds and validated them experimentally.
  • IoT-J workflow optimization: up to 18× faster inference, 16.5× higher throughput, and 30% lower GPU/memory usage.
  • TensorRT precision study: FP16 gave the best speed/accuracy trade-off; INT8 was fastest for real-time perception.
  • Published in ACM TECS, IEEE IoT-J, and IEEE MOST.
